NBER article on Chinese Graduate Students

A thought-provoking study. “The researchers conclude that American universities missed out on a singular opportunity to boost mathematical knowledge because, had they accepted more doctoral students in response to the increased supply of Chinese applicants, the gains of Chinese professors would not have come at the expense of non-Chinese faculty.” To me this paper contributes to the theory that mathematical research is a highly social process; communication and the sharing of ideas are crucial to its success.

Wikipedia's Pokemon Test

The Wikimedia blog reflected on the effect Pokemon has had on encyclopaedic knowledge in a recent post. Over its 20 year history, the Pokemon franchise has supplied us with an ever-growing heap of trivia, factoids, and backstories just waiting to be documented by the public---a cause for concern among “Exclusionists” in the Wikipedia community.

I am generally on the side of the exclusionists in this matter. Plenty of well-kept wikis for fans (see Bulbapedia) exist and seem fit for this purpose. Admittedly, these sites vary wildly in quality and scope (not to mention the advertisement infestation). Wikipedia has perhaps found a compromise by employing its love for comprehensive lists. Will this be an effective long term solution?

The Civic Customers

LA times reported on an app that promotes political transparency---to the granularity of a bar code. I applaud BuyPartisan’s efforts to help enlighten consumers and, unlike the LA times, am not worried about the possibility of increased polarization due to apps like this. If anything, they will help citizens understand that corporations are not simply partisan, they are self-interested agents that use political donations as a means to profit maximize. A general rule of thumb: I am more concerned with the absolute dollar amount that a company devotes to politics, not necessarily its shade of red or blue. Semi-related links here and here.

As a side note, perhaps BuyPartisan could itself become more transparent by shifting to an open source model?
